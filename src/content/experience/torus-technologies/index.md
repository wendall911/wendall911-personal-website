Torus Technologies was a consulting and community development business. I started to promote sustainable community software in Sandpoint, Idaho. Like many rural areas in the United States, Sandpoint, Idaho, is poor and lacks financial resources. Most small communities depend on software and information technologies primarily developed and sold by businesses and institutions outside the local community. Software licensing fees for many individuals, schools, organizations, businesses, and government agencies come at the expense of local services and fail to benefit local businesses. Open source software provides local businesses and individuals with software reliability and quality through a worldwide peer review process of all source code. Our economy grows stronger by keeping the software cycle of development, testing, and deployment in the public domain. The money not spent with proprietary software vendors stays in the local economy.

Aside from consulting, Torus Technologies provided web application development services for hundreds of clients around the globe. It designed, developed, and delivered e-learning applications, embedded software, web applications, websites, domain integration, brochures, catalogs, video productions, security auditing and prevention, SEO/SEM, web servers for mobile apps, CBT, e-commerce, responsive design, campaign emails, landing pages, and micro-sites.
