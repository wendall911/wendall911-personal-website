I started an EdTech company called How2CDROM that produced Multimedia CDROM Educational products for a home improvement how-to line of courses. The target business demographic was home improvement stores like Home Depot and Lowes. I successfully built a fully functional product that generated revenue through a minor contract with Lowes. The project never secured enough outside funding to continue. I don't feel like the project was a complete bust, but more of a break-even project. I didn't incur any debt, but the startup was unsuccessful as a venture. I am grateful for this project, as it was my first go at a traditional startup under our business incubator program. It taught me a lot about working with investors and running a startup from scratch where I'm the visionary and founder.
