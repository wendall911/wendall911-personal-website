My first few months at ETS were very intense. As an individual contributor and a primary author for a new federal contract proposal, I quickly learned the history and scope of [NAEP](https://nces.ed.gov/nationsreportcard/). Given that this was an entirely new contract for the research division at ETS, our team of experts needed to research and thoroughly vet every piece of the proposal. This work resulted in a 430-page federal contract proposal, leading to a $50M+ award from [NCES](https://nces.ed.gov/).

Upon award, I immediately transitioned into a role as a leader on the contract transition team. During the transition, the previous vendor, while legally obligated, presented some challenges in transferring knowledge to my team. Given the obscurity, it was necessary to take a more direct approach and attempt to reimplement the previous process independently. I successfully reverse-engineered parts of the complex processes. I reimplemented them as a part of the ETS infrastructure while working with the engineering team to implement an end-to-end solution fully.
