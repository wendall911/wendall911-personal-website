I scheduled a coffee meeting after a chance encounter with the CEO of a local EdTech company, Essential Education. He introduced me to his company's product, The GED Academy, which he had created out of frustration with the adult education system in the US. The issue he was having was that while the product idea was sound, the development had been managed by utilizing several outside software vendors who lacked an understanding of the needs for an educational product, leading to difficulty in growing sales. The product had great potential but would need significant investment in improving the technology staff and the overall content quality of the education materials. After our first meeting, I believed the owner, already saddled with significant debt, could not afford to hire me. A few days later, I received a call from the owner. He had secured enough funding to pay for a full year of employment and wanted me to come on board and revitalize the company and product, which I excitedly accepted.

During the year, I rearchitected all infrastructure and rewrote critical systems, implementing new APIs for scaling. A scalable infrastructure and vastly improved product enabled the organization to increase sales and become financially stable.
